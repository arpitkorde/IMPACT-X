# Roadmap

This roadmap shows where IMPACT-X is going and what the community is being invited to build. It is a living document — updated as the project evolves.

---

*April 2026*

- [x] Core framework documentation (Human, Agent, Combined layers)
- [x] Work Zone Mapping methodology
- [x] Fairness Protocol
- [x] Basic scoring tool (web calculator)
- [x] Contribution and governance structure
- [x] Case study template
- [x] Five vertical folders (Technology, Healthcare, Education, Consulting, Manufacturing)

## Version 1.0 — Standard
*Target: Early 2027*

Version 1.0 will be declared when:
- The framework has been validated through at least 50 real-world pilots
- The scoring formulas have been statistically tested against outcome data
- At least 5 verticals have complete, community-maintained adaptations
- The Agent Registry contains scores for at least 100 tools across multiple task types
- A peer-reviewed research paper has been submitted validating the Combined IMPACT methodology

This is the version we submit to HR standards bodies and offer to organisations as a production-ready implementation.

---

## Long-Term Vision

IMPACT-X ultimately aims to provide:

1. **An open benchmark for human-AI team performance** — the first independent, methodology-based score of how AI tools affect human work outcomes in practice, not in controlled benchmarks

2. **A policy instrument** — anonymised aggregate data that governments and researchers can use to understand how AI is actually changing work, wages, and contribution at the ground level

3. **A new professional vocabulary** — a shared language for talking about contribution in an AI-augmented world that practitioners, managers, and organisations can use without a consultant in the room

---

## What We Are Explicitly Not Building

- A surveillance tool. IMPACT-X measures contribution, not behaviour.
- A replacement for human judgment. Scores inform conversations — they do not replace them.
- A proprietary standard. This will always be open source, freely available, and community-governed.
